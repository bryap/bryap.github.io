About Me: Brya Kim Patterson

(pronounced br-eye-ah)

Born and raised in the Pacific Northwest, I am a data professional with a passion for turning complex information into actionable insights. I hold an MS in Information Management from the University of Washington and a BS in General Science, Computer Science, and Ethics from the University of Oregon.

My background combines technical rigor in Data Science and Business Intelligence with a foundational understanding of computer science concepts. I thrive at the intersection of data and strategy, specializing in cleaning messy, real-world datasets to build predictive models that drive smarter business decisions. I am a lifelong learner who finds the most value in the nuance of a problem; I enjoy leading with interrogative questions to ensure the "why" behind the data is as clear as the "what." My goal is to make data accessible and interpretable for stakeholders at every level.

I am a dedicated servant leader. I am currently leading AI and Technology initiatives for Self Enchnacement Inc., a non-profit focused on uplifting marginalized commuities in Portland, OR. I also have been serving as a board memeber for the P.I.N.K Ivy Foundation since 2022, leading programs and service projects in support of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc.'s serivce initiatives. In my role as a Scrum Master at Block, Inc., I developed a sprint framework that empowered individual contributors to practice Scrum values, experience true autonomy in their decision-making, and collectively define what quality looks like across the team.

I am currently based in Portland, Oregon. When I’m not diving into datasets, you can find me practicing fiber arts, maintaining my aquariums, or lost in a good book or video game. I’m always open to discussing data ethics, new emerging trends and technologies, or the best RPGs of the year. Let's connect!
